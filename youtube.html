Beginner

A healthy person who has not been trained.
Has enough strength needed to maintain daily life.
Novice

A person who has been trained for 3 months ~ 1 year.
Has strength for intense movements.
Intermediate

A person who has been trained for about 2 years.
Has power to execute special workouts.
Advanced

An athlete who has been trained for several years.
Can compete with professionals.
Elite

A person specialized in weightlifting.
Only certain amount of chosen people can get to this level.

Beginner

As a beginner, it's important to perfect your forms while exercising for later on.
Your routine will focus on getting your form right and building basic strength.


Novice - Intermediate

In this level, you should try out different famous routines and find out which one fits you the most.
After you've found a routine, stick to it until you notice your lift & muscle gains are getting slower,
then you could move onto other routines and repeat.


Advanced +

At this point, you should have good enough experience & body & knowledge about weightlifting to guide yourself.
You could try out programs used by professional weightlifter & bodybuilders, or you can even make your own program!
